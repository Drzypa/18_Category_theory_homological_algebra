Let $\mathcal{A}$ be an abelian category.  The {\em snake lemma} consists of the following two claims:
\begin{enumerate}
\item
Suppose
\[\begin{CD}
0@>>> A_1@>>> B_1@>>> C_1@>>> 0\\
& & @V\alpha VV @V\beta VV @V\gamma VV\\
0@>>> A_2@>>>B_2@>>>C_2@>>>0
\end{CD}\]
is a commutative diagram in $\mathcal{A}$ with exact rows.
Then there is an exact sequence
\[
0 \to   \ker\alpha \to   \ker\beta \to   \ker\gamma
               \stackrel{s}{\longrightarrow}
      \coker\alpha \to \coker\beta \to \coker\gamma\to 0,
\]
usually called the {\em kernel-cokernel sequence}.  The morphism $s$ is
called the {\em connecting morphism}.

\item
Applying the previous claim inductively, for any short exact sequence
\[
0 \to \mathbf{A} \to \mathbf{B} \to \mathbf{C} \to 0
\]
of chain complexes in $\mathcal{A}$, there is a corresponding
\PMlinkname{long exact sequence in homology}{LongExactSequenceOfHomologyGroups}
\[
\cdots \to H_n(\mathbf{A})\to H_n(\mathbf{B})\to H_n(\mathbf{C})
       \to H_{n-1}(\mathbf A)\to\cdots
\]
\end{enumerate}
