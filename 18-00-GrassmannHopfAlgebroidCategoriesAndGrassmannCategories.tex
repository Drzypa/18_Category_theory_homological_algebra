\textbf{Grassmann-Hopf Algebroid Categories and Grassmann Categories}

\begin{definition}
The categories whose objects are either \emph{Grassmann-Hopf al/gebras}, or in general $G-H$ algebroids,
and whose morphisms are \emph{$G-H$ homomorphisms} are called \emph{Grassmann-Hopf Algebroid Categories}. 
\end{definition}
\med

Although carrying a similar name, a quite different type of Grassmann categories have been
introduced previously:

\begin{definition}
\emph{Grassmann Categories} (as in \cite{BM65}) are defined \emph{on $k$ letters over nontrivial abelian categories} $\mathbf{\A}$ as \emph{full subcategories} of the categories $F_{\mathbf{\A}}(x_1,...,x_k)$ consisting of diagrams satisfying the relations: $x_i x_j + x_j x_i = 0$ and $ x_i x_i = 0 $ with additional conditions on coadjoints, coproducts and morphisms. 

    They were shown to be equivalent to the category of right modules over the endomorphism ring of the 
coadjoint $S(R)$ which is isomorphic to the Grassmann--or exterior--ring over $R$ on $k$ letters $E_R(X_1,..., X_N)$.
\end{definition}
