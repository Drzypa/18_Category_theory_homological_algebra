If $X$ is a topological space and $S$ is a subset of $X$, a sheaf $\mathcal F$ on $X$ is said to be \PMlinkescapetext{\emph{acyclic on S}} if
$$
H^q(S,\mathcal F)=0
$$
for all $q\ge 1$.

A sheaf is \PMlinkescapetext{\emph{acyclic}} if it is \PMlinkescapetext{acyclic} on $X$ itself.
