Let $\mathcat{Set}$ be the category of all sets with functions as the morphisms,
and let $\mathcat{Cat}$ be the category of all small categories with functors as the morphisms.

The \textbf{nerve} of a (small) category $C$ is the simplicial set $\hom(i(-),C)$,
where $i\colon \Delta \to \mathcat{Cat}$ is the fully faithful functor
that takes each ordered set $[n]$ in the simplicial category, $\Delta$,
to the pre-order $\mathcat{n+1}$.
The nerve is a functor $\mathcat{Cat} \to \mathcat{Set}^{\Delta^\op}$.

\begin{example}[Nerve of an open covering]
Let $X$ be a topological space with open cover $\{U_\alpha\}$.
The nerve of the open covering of $X$ is the nerve of the partially-ordered set $\{U_\alpha\}$ with relation that of inclusion.
Thus, it assigns to every $n$ the set of maps from the totally ordered set $n+1$ to the poset $\{U_\alpha\}$.
\end{example}
