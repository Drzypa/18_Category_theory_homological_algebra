commutative diagram


Definition 1.

Let 𝒞 be a category. A diagram in 𝒞 is a directed graph Γ with vertex set V and edge set E, (“loops” and “parallel edges” are allowed) together with two maps o:V→Obj⁢(𝒞), m:E→Morph⁢(𝒞) such that if e∈E has source s⁢(e)∈V and target t⁢(e)∈V then m⁢(e)∈Hom𝒞⁢(o⁢(s⁢(e)),o⁢(t⁢(e))).

Usually diagrams are denoted by drawing the corresponding graph and labeling its vertices (respectively edges) with their images under o (respectively m), for example if f:A→B is a morphism
