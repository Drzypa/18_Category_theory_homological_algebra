equivalent definition of a representable functor


We provide an equivalent, motivating, way of defining a representable functor.

Let 𝒞 be a category and F:𝒞→S⁢e⁢t be a covariant functor and A∈𝒞. Then the following are equivalent

  1. 1.

    𝒞⁢(A,-) is naturally isomorphic to F (or, isomorphic in the appropriate category of functors)

  2. 2.

    There exists an element i∈F⁢(A) such that for every B∈𝒞,r∈F⁢(B) there exists a unique f∈𝒞⁢(A,B) such that F⁢(f)⁢(i)=r

To illustrate the significance of this, consider the category 𝒞=𝐕𝐞𝐜𝐭𝐤 of vector spaces over a field k. For arbitrary vector spaces V,W consider the functor F:𝒞→S⁢e⁢t determined by

F⁢(U)=Bilin⁡(V×W,U)

Where this denotes the set of maps which are linear in both entries. This is a covariant functor in the obvious way. Then one may define V⊗W as the object which represents F (if it exists). The significance of the result is it shows this is equivalent to the ’usual’ definition: there is a bilinear map i:V×W→V⊗W through which all bilinear maps from V×W (these are quantified by r in the theorem) factor uniquely. This is because r:V×W→U factors through i exactly when there is an f∈𝒞⁢(V⊗W,U) such that F⁢(f)⁢(i)=r.

Such universal constructions can be shown to be functorial in the basic objects. For instance the tensor product may be shown to be a functor

𝐕𝐞𝐜𝐭𝐤×𝐕𝐞𝐜𝐭𝐤→𝐕𝐞𝐜𝐭𝐤

To generalise this suppose that 𝒟 is a category (roughly representing 𝐕𝐞𝐜𝐭𝐤×𝐕𝐞𝐜𝐭𝐤 in our case) and we have a functor

F:𝒟o⁢p×𝒞→S⁢e⁢t

such that F⁢(d,-):𝒞→S⁢e⁢t is isomorphic to 𝒞⁢(G⁢(d),-) for some object G⁢(d). Then one may show that G extends to a functor in such a way that F⁢(-,-) is naturally isomorphic to 𝒞⁢(G⁢(-),-).

We may show further that if F,F′ are isomorphic functors and G,G′ are functors which represent them respectively, then there is a natural isomorphism between G and G′.
