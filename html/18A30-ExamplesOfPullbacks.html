examples of pullbacks


This entry shows some examples of categorical pullbacks.

  1. 1.

    In the category of sets, the pullback of a pair of functions f:A→C and g:B→C is given by the set D:={(a,b)∈A×B∣f⁢(a)=g⁢(b)}, along with the projections r:D→A and s:D→B. Here’s a sketch of the proof: first, f∘r=g∘s, and if there are functions u:E→A and v:E→B with f∘u=g∘v, then define a function w:E→D by w⁢(e)=(u⁢(e),v⁢(e)). As f⁢(u⁢(e))=g⁢(v⁢(e)), we have that (u⁢(e),v⁢(e))∈D, so that w is a well-defined function. Furthermore, r∘w⁢(e)=r⁢(u⁢(e),v⁢(e))=u⁢(e) and s∘w⁢(e)=s⁢(u⁢(e),v⁢(e))=v⁢(e). Finally, this w is easily seen to be unique. Therefore, (D,r:D→A,s:D→B) is the pullback of f and g.

  2. 2.

    In the category of groups, the pullback of a pair of group homomorphisms f:A→C and g:B→C is again the group D={(a,b)∈A×B∣f⁢(a)=g⁢(b)}, where the product is defined componentwise, along with the usual projections. The verification that this is indeed the pullback of f and g is almost like the one above. The only thing that needs to be verified is that D is indeed a group. If (a,b),(c,d)∈D, then f⁢(a⁢c)=f⁢(a)⁢f⁢(c)=g⁢(b)⁢g⁢(d)=g⁢(b⁢d), so that (a⁢c,b⁢d)∈D. Also, f⁢(1A)=1C=g⁢(1B), so that (1A,1B)∈D. Finally, if (x,y)∈D, then f⁢(x-1)=f⁢(x)-1=g⁢(y)-1=g⁢(y-1), or (x-1,y-1)∈D. Therefore, D is a group (a subgroup of A×B).

  3. 3.

    In fact, both of the examples above can be obtained by finding the equalizer of f∘pA and g∘pB, where pA and pB are projections from A×B to A and B respectively. This is the consequence of the fact that a category with finite products and equalizers also has pullbacks, and the pullbacks are obtained in the manner just described (see proof here (http://planetmath.org/PropertiesOfPullback)).

  4. 4.

    The category of small categories has pullbacks. Given small categories 𝒜,ℬ, and 𝒞, and functors F:𝒜→𝒞 and G:ℬ→𝒞, consider the subcategory 𝒟 of the comma category (F↓G), where

    • –

      objects are (A,B,f) where F⁢(A)=G⁢(B) and f=1F⁢(A), and

    • –

      morphisms are (x,y):(A,B,1F⁢(A))→(C,D,1F⁢(C)) where F⁢(x)=G⁢(y).

    Then it can be shown that 𝒟, along with the the functors

    • –

      H𝒜:𝒟→𝒜 with H𝒜⁢(A,B,f)=A and H𝒜⁢(x,y)=x, and

    • –

      Hℬ:𝒟→ℬ with Hℬ⁢(A,B,f)=B and Hℬ⁢(x,y)=y

    is the pullback of F and G. The proof is similar to the proof on the universal property of a comma category (http://planetmath.org/PropertiesOfACommaCategory).
