diagonal functor


Let 𝒞 be a category. A diagonal functor on 𝒞 is a functor δ:𝒞→𝒞I for some set I given by

δ⁢(A)=(A)i∈I  and  δ⁢(α)=(α)i∈I.

Here, 𝒞I denotes the I-fold direct product (http://planetmath.org/ProductCategory) of the category 𝒞. For any given I, δ is unique.

δ is faithful (http://planetmath.org/FaithfulFunctor). Its image, δ⁢(𝒞), is the subcategory of 𝒞I whose objects are (A)i∈I and morphisms are (α)i∈I. δ⁢(𝒞) is isomorphic (http://planetmath.org/CategoryIsomorphism) to 𝒞, and may be pictured as the great diagonal of an I-dimensional “cube”.

More generally, when I is a category, then the diagonal functor is just a functor δ that sends each object A∈𝒞 to the constant functor δ⁢(A):I→𝒞 with fixed value A, and every morphism α:A→B to the natural transformation δ⁢(α):δ⁢(A)⁢→˙⁢δ⁢(B), which sends every object i∈I to α. A routine verification shows that δ⁢(α) is indeed a natural transformation.
