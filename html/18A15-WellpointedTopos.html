well-pointed topos


The concept of well-pointed topoi was introduced by Freyd in [1]. A topos is well-pointed if it satisfies either the following equivalent conditions:

  1. 1.

    The terminal object 1 distinguishes morphisms in the sense that if the diagram

    \xymatrix⁢1⁢\ar⁢[r]x⁢&⁢A⁢\ar⁢@⁢<1⁢e⁢x>⁢[r]f⁢\ar⁢@⁢<-1⁢e⁢x>⁢[r]g⁢&⁢B

    commutes for every morphism x:1→A, then in fact

    \xymatrix⁢A⁢\ar⁢@⁢<1⁢e⁢x>⁢[r]f⁢\ar⁢@⁢<-1⁢e⁢x>⁢[r]g⁢&⁢B

    commutes, that is, f=g. Moreover, 1 is not isomorphic to the initial object.

  2. 2.

    The topos 𝒯 is complemented and supports split, and the truth object Ω of 𝒯 has exactly two elements, ⊤:1→Ω, and ⊥:1→Ω. To say that 𝒯 is complemented means that if m:X→Y is a monomorphism, then there exists a monomorphism m′:X′→Y such that m⊔m′:X⊔X′→Y is an isomorphism. To say that 𝒯 supports split means that every subobject of 1 is projective.

Every well-pointed topos is a Boolean topos.
