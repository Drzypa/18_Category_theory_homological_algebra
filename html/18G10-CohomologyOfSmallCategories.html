cohomology of small categories


Let 𝒞 be a small category. For n≥0 we have functors Δn:𝒞→Ab which send an object X∈𝒞 to the free abelian group generated by n+1-tuples of morphisms to X. The action of Δn on a morphism f:X→Y is defined by:

Δn⁢(f):(g0,g1,⋯,gn)↦(f⁢g0,f⁢g1,⋯,f⁢gn)

for any morphisms g0,g1,⋯,gn∈𝒞 with codomain X.

For n>0 the natural transformation ∂n:Δn→Δn-1 is defined by letting the homomorphism [∂n]X:Δn⁢(X)→Δn-1⁢(X) be given by:

[∂n]X⁢(f0,f1,⋯,fn)
=(f1,⋯⁢fn)-(f0,f2,⋯,fn)+⋯+[-1n]⁢(f0,f1,⋯,fn-1)

Hence we have a of natural transformations:

⋯→∂n+1Δn→∂nΔn-1→∂n-1⋯→∂2Δ1→∂1Δ0

For any functor F:𝒞→Ab, let [Δn,F] denote the abelian group of natural transformations Δn→F. Also let ∂n:[Δn-1,F]→[Δn,F] denote the abelian group homomorphism sending η→η⁢∂n.

We have a chain complex:

⋯←∂n+1[Δn,F]←∂n[Δn-1,F]←∂n-1⋯←∂2[Δ1,F]←∂1[Δ0,F]

It is easily verified that H0⁢([Δ*,F],∂*) is just lim←⁢(F), the inverse limit of F. This motivates the definition:

lim←n⁢(F)=Hn⁢([Δ*,F],∂*)

Note that if 𝒞 is a group G (that is 𝒞 has one object and all its morphisms are invertible) then F may be regarded as a module M, over G. In this case lim←n⁢(F) coincides with group cohomology: lim←n⁢(F)=Hn⁢(G;M).
