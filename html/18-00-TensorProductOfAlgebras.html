tensor product of algebras


Let A and B be algebras over a commutative ring R. As modules, we can form the tensor product A⊗B. The resulting structure is again an R-module. Since A and B have the additional structure of being algebras, we would like this structure being preserved in A⊗B as well. This can indeed be done, if we define a “multiplication” ⋅:(A⊗B)×(A⊗B)→A⊗B by:

(a⊗b)⋅(c⊗d):=a⁢c⊗b⁢d,a,c∈A⁢ and ⁢b,d∈B

and requiring that ⋅ distributes over +, that is,

x⋅(y+z):=x⋅y+x⋅z,(y+z)⋅x:=y⋅x+z⋅x,x,y,z∈A⊗B.

With this and the fact that ⋅ is associative, (A⊗B,+,⋅) becomes a ring. To turn A⊗B into an R-algebra, we need to verify that ⋅ is bilinear, that is:

r⁢(x⋅y)=(r⁢x)⋅y=x⋅(r⁢y)  x,y∈A⊗B⁢ and ⁢r∈R.

Because of the distributivity of ⋅ over +, it is enough to verify the case when x=a⊗b and y=c⊗d. Then r⁢((a⊗b)⋅(c⊗d))=r⁢(a⁢c⊗b⁢d)=(r⁢a⁢c)⊗b⁢d=(r⁢a⊗b)⋅(c⊗d)=(r⁢(a⊗b))⋅(c⊗d). This shows that r⁢(x⋅y)=(r⁢x)⋅y. Since (r⁢a⁢c)⊗b⁢d=a⁢c⊗(r⁢b⁢d), we also have r⁢(x⋅y)=x⋅(r⁢y). Therefore, A⊗B is an algebra over R.

Here are some basic properties of the tensor product of algebras:

  • •

    if A and B are both unital, so is A⊗B, with 1⊗1 as the multiplicative identity

  • •

    if A and B are both commutative, so is A⊗B

  • •

    A⊗B≅B⊗A

  • •

    (A⊗B)⊗C≅A⊗(B⊗C)

  • •

    (A⊕B)⊗C≅(A⊗C)⊕(B⊗C)

  • •

    R⊗A≅A

  • •

    Assume both A and B are unital algebras, the canonical injections A→A⊗B and B→A⊗B, given by

    a↦a⊗1,b↦1⊗b

    turn A,B into subalgebras of A⊗B (up to algebra isomorphism). In fact, A and B are commuting subalgebras, in the sense that

    (A⊗1)⋅(1⊗B)=(1⊗B)⋅(A⊗1).

Like tensor products of modules, there is also a universal property associated with the tensor products of algebras: let A and B be R-algebras and f:A→C and g:B→C be algebra homomorphisms such that f⁢(A)⁢g⁢(B)=g⁢(B)⁢f⁢(A) and f⁢(R⋅1)=g⁢(R⋅1). Then there is a unique R-algebra D (=A⊗B) and algebra homomorphism h:D→C, such that the following diagram of algebra homomorphisms commutes:

\xymatrix⁢A⁢\ar⁢[d⁢r]f⁢\ar⁢[d]⁢&⁢D⁢\ar⁢[r]h⁢&⁢C⁢B⁢\ar⁢[u⁢r]g⁢\ar⁢[u]⁢&
