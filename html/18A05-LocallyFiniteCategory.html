locally finite category


A locally finite category is a category 𝒞 such that each arrow (http://planetmath.org/Category) can be written as a composition of non-identity arrows in only finitely many ways. To get a picture of what this means, view 𝒞 as a graph, where the nodes are objects and the directed edges are all the arrows besides the identity arrows. Note that loops besides identity arrows are permitted. Then a decomposition of an arrow f:A→B is a directed path from A to B in the graph. So the statement that each arrow can be decomposed in only finitely many ways essentially means that there are only finitely many directed paths from A to B in the graph. This analogy only breaks down in the case where A and B are the same object.

The notion of a locally finite category generalizes the notion of a locally finite poset. The condition that each interval [x,z] in a poset P contain only finitely many elements y of P implies that a locally finite poset is locally finite when viewed as a category, since between two objects of P there is at most one arrow.

If 𝒞 is a locally finite category, then an algebra over the category (http://planetmath.org/AlgebraFormedFromACategory) arises as the dual of a coalgebra over the category.
