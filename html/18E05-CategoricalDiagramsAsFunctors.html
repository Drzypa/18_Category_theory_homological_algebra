categorical diagrams as functors


0.1 Introduction: categorical diagrams defined by functors

Any categorical diagram can be defined via a corresponding functor (associated with a diagram as shown by Mitchell, 1965, in ref. [1]). Such functors associated with diagrams are very useful in the categorical theory of representations as in the case of categorical algebra (http://planetmath.org/CategoricalAlgebra). As a particuarly useful example in (commutative) homological algebra let us consider the case of an exact categorical sequence that has a correspondingly defined exact functor introduced for example in Abelian category theory.
