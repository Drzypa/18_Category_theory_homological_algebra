natural equivalence of categories


Definition 0.1.

Let us consider two arbitrary categories 𝒞 and 𝒟. A natural equivalence of categories* is said to exist between two categories 𝒞 and 𝒟 if and only if there is a covariant functor E:𝒞→𝒟 which is full and faithful, and that also has a full and faithful adjoint (that is either a left- or right- adjoint).

* See ref. [288] in the bibliography for category theory and algebraic topology (http://planetmath.org/CategoricalOntologyABibliographyOfCategoryTheory).

0.0.1 Examples:

  1. 1.

    Category of cross modules of groups, and the category of categorical groups (http://planetmath.org/NaturalEquivalenceOfC_GAndC_MCategories)

  2. 2.

    Category 𝐦𝐨𝐝𝐁 of finite-dimensional right-B modules, and the category 𝒞/Σ⁢T of ideals of morphisms of a category 𝒞 which factor through a direct some of ideal copies Σ⁢T.

  3. 3.

    The category of crossed modules of R–algebroids is equivalent to the category of double R–algebroids with thin structure (Brown and Mosa, 1986, 2008.)

  4. 4.

    The categories of crossed modules of algebroids and of double algebroids with a connection pair are equivalent.
