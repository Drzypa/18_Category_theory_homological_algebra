image of a morphism


Image

Let 𝒞 be a category and a f:A→B a morphism from objects A to B in 𝒞. An image of f is a subobject I of B with a representing monomorphism g:I→B, such that

  • •

    f factors through g; i.e. there is a morphism h:A→I such that f=g∘h:

    A⟶fB=A⟶hI⟶gB
  • •

    if f factors through a monomorphism s:J→B:

    A⟶fB=A⟶tJ⟶sB,

    then there is a morphism x:I→J such that

    I⟶gB=I⟶xJ⟶sB.

Example. In the category of sets, the image of a function f:A→B is just the image (http://planetmath.org/Image) f⁢(A) with the canonical injection into B.

In the literature, the first bullet is equivalent to saying that the subobject g:C→B allows f. So the definition of the image of f is the smallest subobject of B that allows f:A→B.

Coimage

Dually, one defines the coimage of f:A→B as a quotient object C of A with a representing epimorphism h:A→C, such that

  • •

    f factors through h; i.e. there is a morphism g:C→B such that f=g∘h:

    A⟶fB=A⟶hC⟶gB
  • •

    if f factors through an epimorphism t:A→D:

    A⟶fB=A⟶tD⟶sB,

    then there is a morphism y:D→C such that

    A⟶hC=A⟶tD⟶yC.

Remarks.

  1. 1.

    In the definition of image, since g is a monomorphism, x is a monomorphism. Furthermore, since s is a monomorphism, x is uniquely determined, and we have the following commutative diagram:

    \xymatrix⁢@⁢R-=2⁢p⁢t⁢&⁢I⁢\ar⁢[d⁢d]x⁢\ar⁢[d⁢r]g⁢&⁢A⁢\ar⁢[u⁢r]h⁢\ar⁢[d⁢r]t⁢&⁢&⁢B⁢&⁢J⁢\ar⁢[u⁢r]s⁢&

  2. 2.

    Dually, y is a uniquely determined epimorphism satisfying the following commutative diagram:

    \xymatrix⁢@⁢R-=2⁢p⁢t⁢&⁢D⁢\ar⁢[d⁢d]y⁢\ar⁢[d⁢r]g⁢&⁢A⁢\ar⁢[u⁢r]t⁢\ar⁢[d⁢r]h⁢&⁢&⁢B⁢&⁢C⁢\ar⁢[u⁢r]s⁢&

  3. 3.

    If f:A→B has an image (dually, coimage), it is unique up to isomorphism. The image and coimage of f are denoted by im⁡(f) and coim⁡(f) respectively.

  4. 4.

    Suppose that a category is Ab1. If im⁡(f) and coim⁡(f) exist for f:A→B, then there is a unique morphism f¯:coim⁡(f)→im⁡(f) such that

    \xymatrix⁢@⁢C+=4⁢p⁢c⁢A⁢\ar⁢[r]f⁢\ar⁢[d]⁢&⁢B⁢coim⁡(f)⁢\ar⁢[r]f¯⁢&⁢im⁡(f)⁢\ar⁢[u]

    is commutative. The Ab2 Axiom, a la Grothendieck, is the statement that if f¯ exists, it is an isomorphism. A category is said to be Ab2 if it is Ab1, and every morphism satisfies the Ab2 Axiom.

  5. 5.

    A category 𝒞 is said to have images (dually, has coimages) if the image (coimage) of any morphism exists.

Every abelian category has images and coimages, and

im⁡(f)=ker⁡(cok⁡(f))   and   coim⁡(f)=cok⁡(ker⁡(f)),

where ker and cok are the kernel and cokernel operations. In addition, we have the following important result:

if a morphism f:A→B can be factored as A⟶fB=A⟶hI⟶gB with g a monomorphism and h an epimorphism, then I (with g) is the image of f and I (with h) is the coimage of f.

In other words, the factorization above is uniquely determined, up to isomorphism.
