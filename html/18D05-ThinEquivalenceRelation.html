thin equivalence relation


1 Thin equivalence relation

Definition 1.1.

Let a,a′:x≃y be paths in X. Then a is thinly equivalent to a′, denoted a∼Ta′, if there is a thin relative homotopy between a and a′.

We note that ∼T is an equivalence relation, see [2]. We use ⟨a⟩:x≃y to denote the ∼T class of a path a:x≃y and call ⟨a⟩ the semitrack of a. The groupoid structure of 𝝆1□⁢(X) is induced by concatenation, +, of paths. Here one makes use of the fact that if a:x≃x′,a′:x′≃x′′,a′′:x′′≃x′′′ are paths then there are canonical thin relative homotopies

(a+a′)+a′′≃a+(a′+a′′):x≃x′′′⁢(𝑟𝑒𝑠𝑐𝑎𝑙𝑒)a+ex′≃a:x≃x′;ex+a≃a:x≃x′⁢(𝑑𝑖𝑙𝑎𝑡𝑖𝑜𝑛)a+(-a)≃ex:x≃x⁢(𝑐𝑎𝑛𝑐𝑒𝑙𝑙𝑎𝑡𝑖𝑜𝑛).

The source and target maps of 𝝆1□⁢(X) are given by

∂1-⁡⟨a⟩=x,∂1+⁡⟨a⟩=y,

if ⟨a⟩:x≃y is a semitrack. Identities and inverses are given by

ε⁢(x)=⟨ex⟩ resp.-⟨a⟩=⟨-a⟩.
