forgetful functor


Let 𝒞 and 𝒟 be categories such that each object c of 𝒞 can be regarded an object of 𝒟 by suitably ignoring structures c may have as a 𝒞-object but not a 𝒟-object. A functor U:𝒞→𝒟 which operates on objects of 𝒞 by “forgetting” any imposed mathematical structure is called a forgetful functor. The following are examples of forgetful functors:

  1. 1.

    U:𝐆𝐫𝐩→𝐒𝐞𝐭 takes groups into their underlying sets and group homomorphisms to set maps.

  2. 2.

    U:𝐓𝐨𝐩→𝐒𝐞𝐭 takes topological spaces into their underlying sets and continuous maps to set maps.

  3. 3.

    U:𝐀𝐛→𝐆𝐫𝐩 takes abelian groups to groups and acts as identity on arrows.

Forgetful functors are often instrumental in studying adjoint functors.
