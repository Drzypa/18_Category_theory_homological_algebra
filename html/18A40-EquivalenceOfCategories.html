equivalence of categories


Let C and D be two categories with functors F:C→D and G:D→C. The functors F and G are an equivalence of categories if there are natural isomorphisms F⁢G≅idD and G⁢F≅idC.

Note, F is left adjoint to G, and G is right adjoint to F as

homD⁡(F⁢(c),d)⟶GhomC⁡(G⁢F⁢(c),G⁢(d))⟷homC⁡(c,G⁢(d)).

And, F is right adjoint to G, and G is left adjoint to F as

homC⁡(G⁢(d),c)⟶FhomD⁡(F⁢G⁢(d),F⁢(c))⟷homD⁡(d,F⁢(c)).

In practical terms, two categories are equivalent if there is a fully (http://planetmath.org/FullFunctor) faithful functor F:C→D, such that every object d∈D is isomorphic to an object F⁢(c), for some c∈C.
