Theorem 1.

Every small abelian category admits an exact (http://planetmath.org/ExactFunctor) and full (http://planetmath.org/FullFunctor) embedding (http://planetmath.org/FaithfulFunctor) into the category ùêåùê®ùêùR of (left) modules over some ring R.

As a consequence, this theorem says that certain facts about small abelian categories can be proved in the more concrete setting of ModR (indeed a concrete category). For example, in order to prove that a sequence is exact in an abelian category, it is enough to prove it in the context of ModR, by realizing the fact that objects in ModR are sets (with structures) and utilizing the elements therein. In particular, the diagram chasing technique popular in homological algebra may be formulated in small abelian categories as a result of this theorem.
