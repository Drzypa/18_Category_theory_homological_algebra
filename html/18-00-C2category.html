C2-category


In general, a C2-category is an 𝒜⁢b⁢4-category, or, alternatively, an 𝒜⁢b⁢3- and 𝒜⁢b⁢3* -category C∗ with certain additional conditions for the canonical morphism from direct sums to products of any family of objects in 𝒞 [2]).

Definition 0.1.

A C2-category is defined as a category 𝒞 that has products, coproducts and a zero object, and if the morphism ι:⊕Ai→𝐗⁢Ai is a monomorphism for any family of objects {Ai} in 𝒞 (p. 81 in [1]).

Remark 0.1.

One readily obtains the result that a C2-category is C1 ([1]).
