category of C*-algebras


Definition 0.1.

Let 𝒜,ℬ be two C*-algebras (http://planetmath.org/CAlgebra3). Then a *-homomorphism ϕ*:𝒜⟶ℬ is defined as a C*-algebra homomorphism ϕ:𝒜→ℬ which respects involutions, that is:

ϕ⁢(a*𝒜)=ϕ⁢(a)*ℬ, for any ⁢a∈𝒜.
Remark 0.1.

If ‘by abuse of notation’ one uses * to denote both *𝒜 and *ℬ, then any *-homomorphism ϕ commutes with *, i.e., ϕ*=*ϕ. Homomorphisms between C*-algebras are automatically continuous (http://planetmath.org/ContinuousLinearMapping).

Definition 0.2.

The category 𝒞 whose objects are C*-algebras and whose morphisms are *-homomorphisms is called the category of C*-algebras or the C*-algebra category.
