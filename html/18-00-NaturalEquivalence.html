natural equivalence


Let F,G:𝒞→𝒟 be a pair of functors from the category 𝒞 to the category 𝒟. A natural transformation between functors τ:F→G is called a natural equivalence (or a natural isomorphism) if there is a natural transformation σ:G→F such that τ∙σ=idG and σ∙τ=idF where idF is the identity natural transformation on F, and composition ∙ is the usual (vertical) composition on natural transformations.

Equivalently, one can define a natural equivalence from functors F to G to be a natural transformation τ such that for each object A in 𝒞, the morphism τA:F⁢(A)→G⁢(A) is an isomorphism in 𝒟.
