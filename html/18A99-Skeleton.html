Remark. From the example above, we see that there may be more than one skeleton in any given category. However, as natural equivalence is an equivalence relation on the class of subcategories of a category, any two skeletons are naturally equivalent, since they are both naturally equivalent to the original category. Therefore, we may think of a skeleton as uniquely defined by the category, and this uniqueness is understood to be up to natural equivalence. In summary, any two skeletons of a category are isomorphic.
