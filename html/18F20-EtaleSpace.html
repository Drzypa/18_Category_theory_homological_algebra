Étalé space


The Étalé space (Espace Étalé) is a topological space associated to a presheaf ℱ on a space X. The Étalé space is defined to be the disjoint union of stalks of the sheaf ℱ.

Over each open set U⊂X, there is a set of sections Γ⁢(U,ℱ). A basis for the topology on the Étalé space is formed by taking the open sets to be of the form 𝒰s={sx,x∈U}, for s∈Γ⁢(U,ℱ) and sx the germ of s at x. There is a natural map π:ℰℱ→X which takes germs sx in the stalk ℱx over x to x.

Let s∈Γ⁢(U,ℱ) and s′∈Γ⁢(U′,ℱ) with U∩U′≠∅. At each point x∈U∩U′ where sx=sx′, by the definition of germs there exists an open set V⊂U∩U′ containing x such that s and s′ restrict to the same section on V (s|V=s′|V). This verifies that {𝒰s} form a basis for ℰℱ.

Then there is another presheaf, ℱ~, whose sections are the continuous functions from X to ℰℱ assigning an element s⁢(x)∈ℱx to each point x∈X. This presheaf forms a sheaf equivalent to the sheafification of the presheaf ℱ.
