snake lemma


Let 𝒜 be an abelian category. The snake lemma consists of the following two claims:

  1. 1.

    Suppose

    0→A1→B1→C1→0α↓β↓γ↓0→A2→B2→C2→0

    is a commutative diagram in 𝒜 with exact rows. Then there is an exact sequence

    0→ker⁢α→ker⁢β→ker⁢γ⟶scoker⁢α→coker⁢β→coker⁢γ→0,

    usually called the kernel-cokernel sequence. The morphism s is called the connecting morphism.

  2. 2.

    Applying the previous claim inductively, for any short exact sequence

    0→𝐀→𝐁→𝐂→0

    of chain complexes in 𝒜, there is a corresponding long exact sequence in homology (http://planetmath.org/LongExactSequenceOfHomologyGroups)

    ⋯→Hn⁢(𝐀)→Hn⁢(𝐁)→Hn⁢(𝐂)→Hn-1⁢(𝐀)→⋯
