Yoneda lemma


If 𝒞 is a category, write 𝒞^ for the category of contravariant functors from 𝒞 to 𝐒𝐞𝐭𝐬, the category of sets. The morphisms in 𝒞^ are natural transformations of functors.

(To avoid set theoretical concerns, one can take a universe 𝒰 and take all categories to be 𝒰-small.)

For any object X of 𝒞, hX=Hom⁢(-,X) is a contravariant functor from 𝒞 to 𝐒𝐞𝐭𝐬, and therefore is an object of 𝒞^.

Yoneda Lemma says that X↦hX is a covariant functor 𝒞→𝒞^, which embeds 𝒞 faithfully as a full subcategory of 𝒞^. This embedding is called the Yoneda embedding.
