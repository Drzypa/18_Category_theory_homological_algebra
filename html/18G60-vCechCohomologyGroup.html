Čech cohomology group


Let ℱ be a sheaf of abelian groups on a topological space X and consider an 𝒰={Ui}i∈I of X. For the sake of simplicity denote

Ui0⁢i1⁢⋯⁢iq=Ui0∩Ui1∩…∩Uiq.

The group Cˇq⁢(𝒰,ℱ) of Čech q-cochains is the set of families

c=(ci0⁢i1⁢⋯⁢iq)∈∏(i0,…,iq)∈Iq+1ℱ⁢(Ui0⁢i1⁢⋯⁢iq).

The group on Cˇq⁢(𝒰,ℱ) is the obvious one deduced from the addition law on sections of ℱ.

The Čech differential

δq:Cˇq⁢(𝒰,ℱ)→Cˇq+1⁢(𝒰,ℱ)

is defined by the

(δq⁢c)i0⁢⋯⁢iq+1=∑0≤j≤q+1(-1)j⁢ci0⁢⋯⁢ij^⁢⋯⁢iq+1|Ui0⁢⋯⁢iq+1,

and we set Cˇq⁢(𝒰,ℱ)=0, δq=0 for q<0. Easy computations show that δq+1∘δq=0. We get therefore a cochain complex (Cˇ∙⁢(𝒰,ℱ),δ), called the complex of Čech cochains relative to the 𝒰.

The q-th Čech cohomology group of ℱ relative to 𝒰 is

Hˇq⁢(𝒰,ℱ)=Hq⁢(Cˇ∙⁢(𝒰,ℱ)).
