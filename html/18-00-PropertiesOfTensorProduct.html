properties of tensor product


Theorem 1.

Let V1,V2 be two vector spaces over a field F. If x1,…,xn∈V1 are linearly independent and y1,…,yn∈V2 then

∑xi⊗yi=0⇒yi=0, for all ⁢i
Proof.

Take the dual vectors xi* to the vectors xi, i.e. xi*⁢(xj)=δi,j. Given arbitrary linear functionals fi:V2→F, define a bilinear form f:V1×V2→F by

f⁢(x,y)=∑j=1nxj*⁢(x)⁢fj⁢(y)

By the definition of tensor product there exists a unique linear functional ϕ:V1⊗V2→F such that ϕ∘ι=f. Therefore

0 = ϕ⁢(∑ixi⊗yi)
= ∑iϕ∘ι⁢(xi,yi)
= ∑if⁢(xi,yi)
= ∑i∑jxj*⁢(xi)⁢fj⁢(yi)
= ∑ifi⁢(yi).

Since the fi are arbitrary, it follows that yi=0 for all i.

∎
