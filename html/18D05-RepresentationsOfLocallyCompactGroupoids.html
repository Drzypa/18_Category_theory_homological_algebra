representations of locally compact groupoids


Definition 0.1.

Let 𝖦l⁢c be a locally compact (topological) groupoid endowed with a Haar system ν=νu,u∈U𝖦l⁢c. Then a representation of 𝖦l⁢c together with the its associated Haar system ν is defined as a triple (μ,U𝖦l⁢c*ℋ,L), where: μ is a quasi-invariant measure defined over U𝖦l⁢c,

U𝖦l⁢c*ℋ is an analytical, fibered Hilbert space or Hilbert bundle over U𝖦l⁢c, and

L:U𝖦l⁢c⟶𝐈𝐬𝐨⁢(U𝖦l⁢c*ℋ) is a Borelian (or borelian) groupoid morphism whose restriction on U𝖦l⁢c is the identification map, that is, U𝐈𝐬𝐨⁢(U𝖦l⁢c*ℋ) is being identified via L with U𝖦l⁢c. Thus,

L⁢(x)=[r⁢(x),L~⁢(x),d⁢(x)],

where L~⁢(x):ℋ⁢(d⁢(x))⟶ℋ⁢(r⁢(x)) is a Hilbert space ℋ isomorphism.
